### 3. CE engine (joint)

How CE works: psychologists must earn continuing education credits each license cycle (typically 20-40 hours per 2-year cycle, state-specific). Credits only count from APA-approved sponsors or state-approved providers. A vendor cannot self-issue CE; the play is to partner with an approved sponsor who reviews content for educational standards. Rules: content must be genuinely educational, not promotional; commercial support must be disclosed; product demo happens AFTER the CE portion or in follow-up. Costs typically $500-2,000 per event through a sponsor, or revenue share.

Plan:
1. Now: monthly NON-CE webinar ("Defensible use of AI in psychological assessment", forensic and clinical variants), Robert + a clinical advisor presenting. Registration = lead capture.
2. Parallel: approach approved CE sponsors (CONCEPT/Palo Alto University, state associations that sponsor CE, commercial CE platforms) for co-provided CE webinars with disclosure.
3. CE-eligible version becomes the flagship top-of-funnel event, repeatable monthly.

KPI: registrants, attendance rate, trial conversion within 14 days of webinar.

### 6. Channel and partnerships (priority order)

1. **CE providers** (CONCEPT / Palo Alto U, commercial CE platforms, state associations): co-provided CE webinars. Fastest trust transfer available to us. See CE engine.
2. **Association member-benefit deals:** state psychological associations run member discount programs. Offer: members get 10-15% off Pro, association gets rev share or flat sponsorship. Trusted endorsement + newsletter distribution in one deal. Start with 2-3 states where forensic work is dense (CA, TX, FL, CO home state).
3. **Influencer/affiliate program:** 3-5 trusted voices (Jeremy Sharp tier, assessment CE instructors, practice consultants for testing psychologists). 20-25% first-year revenue share, tracked links. In a trust market, borrowed trust is the fastest currency.
4. **Professional liability insurers** (The Trust insures most US psychologists): co-branded "defensible AI use" guidance. Hard to land, category-defining if it works. Content-first approach: send them the whitepaper, offer co-authorship.
5. **Attorney-side channel:** SEAK sponsorship, JurisPro/SEAK expert directories, family law and criminal defense bar CLE content ("what evaluator tooling means for your case"). Attorneys hire evaluators; nobody markets to this side.
6. **Practice consultants and billing services** for testing practices: referral fee arrangement. They advise on tooling; be the answer.
7. **University forensic/clinical psych programs:** free edu licenses for doctoral training clinics. Pipeline play, zero marginal cost (local-first).
8. **Test publishers (PAR, Pearson, MHS, WPS): LATER.** They have competing platform ambitions (Q-global, PARiConnect). Year-one stance: interoperability marketing ("works with score reports from..."), no formal partnership attempts until we have volume.
9. **EHR vendors (SimplePractice, TherapyNotes): LATER.** Different workflow center. Directory listings only for now.

KPI per partnership: signed deals, partner-attributed trials.
