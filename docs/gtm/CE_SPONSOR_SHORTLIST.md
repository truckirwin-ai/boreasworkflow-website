## Notes and pitch angles

**1. CONCEPT / PAU CAPS.** Founded by Patricia Zapf, acquired by PAU in 2018, now the CAPS division. Runs named partner bundles (AAFP, ABPP, NYSAP, Protect International), so a Boreas-sponsored series has precedent. Pitch: "Workflow and quality control in forensic report production" as a live CE webinar with an independent forensic presenter. Top priority.

**2. AAFP.** The credentialing community for board-certified forensic psychologists; its webinar CE runs through CONCEPT, so one relationship can cover both. Pitch: exhibit or sponsor at the Portland 2026 meeting to reach exactly the forensic buyer.

**3. GIFR.** APA approved, forensic focused, already runs free live webinars with partner organizations, which matches a sponsored-education model at low cost. Caution: owned by Multi-Health Systems, a test publisher that may view Boreas as adjacent competition (see plan: test publishers are LATER). Pitch: risk assessment workflow efficiency webinar. Approach with care.

**4. TZK Seminars.** Small, founder-run (Keith Hannan answers the phone), high live-webinar volume with outside presenters; low price structure suggests an affordable revenue-share deal is negotiable. Pitch: recruit a known assessment psychologist to present "Managing the evaluation pipeline", with Boreas as disclosed commercial supporter.

**5. At Health.** APA-approved sponsor that already white-labels CE for The Testing Psychologist podcast, proving it partners with outside content brands. Pitch: replicate the Testing Psychologist model with a Boreas-supported course on evaluation workflow.

**6. The Testing Psychologist.** Jeremy Sharp (Colorado Center for Assessment and Counseling, Fort Collins) has no sponsor status of his own but is the single best audience match: testing psychologists in private practice. Pitch: podcast sponsorship plus a co-created CE episode issued through At Health; also a plausible presenter partner for any sponsor above. Colorado location is a bonus. Ties into the separate Sharp pitch draft.

**7 to 10. State associations.** Colorado (home state, explicitly co-sponsors outside programs), California (formal CE accreditation program, applications about six weeks ahead), Texas (published CE endorsement application), Florida (weakest for live webinars, mostly on-demand, but a named staff contact). State association co-sponsorship fees typically run a few hundred dollars per event, the best match to the $400/mo CE budget line. Pitch to each: member-benefit webinar on documentation efficiency and ethics in high-volume assessment practice.

**Dropped:** PESI (large in-house production machine) and ContinuingEdCourses.net (text-course only). Both verified sponsors, neither fits a small vendor live-webinar play.

## Rules and process pages

- APA co-sponsorship and commercial support FAQs: https://www.apa.org/ed/sponsor/resources/faqs (co-sponsorship is mutual planning, never a transfer of approval; commercial support must be disclosed before registration and at program start)
- APA Standards and Criteria for Approval of Sponsors: https://www.apa.org/about/policy/approval-standards.pdf
- California PA CE accreditation: https://www.cpapsych.org/page/CEApproval
- Texas PA CE endorsement: https://www.texaspsyc.org/ce-endorsement
- Colorado PA CE requirements and approval: https://www.coloradopsych.org/ce-requirements-approval
