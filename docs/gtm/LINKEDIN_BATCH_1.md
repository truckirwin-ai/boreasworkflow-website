## Post 2 (forensic)

A forensic report lives or dies on the witness stand by one question: can you show your work?

That shaped how we built Boreas Workflow. Every diagnostic decision is rendered and signed by the evaluator before a word of the report is drafted, and the full decision trail exports for discovery. Nothing in the report stands as a bare assertion.

The other half: the case file is encrypted on the evaluator's own machine. It is never in our cloud, because we do not have a copy to subpoena.

Defensible by design, not by disclaimer.

## Post 3 (clinical)

The bottleneck in a testing practice is not the testing. It is the writing.

You can administer and score faster than you can write the report. So reports stack up, and the backlog quietly caps how many patients you see and how much the practice bills.

Boreas Workflow takes the scored case file and drafts the report in your voice, criterion-mapped and source-cited the way payer reviewers want it. The writing stops being the throttle.

Patient data stays on your machine the whole time.

If you run assessments, I would value your read: boreasworkflow.com/clinical

## Post 4 (build-in-public)

Every AI tool for clinicians right now is fighting the same instinct: to have the model make the call.

We drew the opposite line and made it architectural. In Boreas, the doctor always diagnoses. The software cannot render a diagnosis. It organizes the case, surfaces the data, and drafts the writing, but the clinical judgment is rendered and signed by a licensed human before anything downstream happens.

That constraint made the product harder to build and easier to trust. I would make the same call again.

## Post 6 (clinical)

A question I keep asking testing psychologists: what would you do with the hours if the report writing were cut in half?

That is the problem Boreas Workflow is pointed at. Not replacing your judgment. Giving you back the time the writing eats.
