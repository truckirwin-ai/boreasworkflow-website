# First-500 Call Script (call-first motion)

**Owner:** Robert (caller). Claude prepares list views and this script.
**Why call-first:** the first_500 list is 100% phone coverage, 0% email. Email enrichment is queued but not landed, so the phone is the only channel that reaches this segment today.
**Segment:** 499 active-licensed individual psychologists (CO/KS/NE), license-anchored, strict subset of the full 7,788.
**Track:** default clinical unless the practice is visibly forensic (court roster, custody, evaluation-heavy), then switch to the forensic frame below.
**Compliance posture:** this is B2B professional outreach to licensees at their listed practice numbers, not consumer telemarketing. Still: identify yourself and the company immediately, honor any do-not-call request on the spot and log it, no calls before 9am or after 5pm local, leave at most one voicemail per contact per week. Never state or imply CE credit, endorsement, or a clinical claim the product does not make.

---

## Before the call (30 seconds)

- Note name, city, license status, and any practice-area hint from the list view.
- Decide track: clinical (throughput/backlog) or forensic (defensibility/court).
- Goal of the call, in order of preference: (1) book a 15-minute demo, (2) get an email to send the demo video + founder link, (3) get permission to follow up. Any of the three is a win. A trial start is a bonus, not the ask.

## Opening (say who you are first)

"Hi, is this [Dr. Last name]? This is Robert Irwin with Boreas Workflow, we build desktop software for psychological evaluation. Do you have about ninety seconds, or is there a better time to reach you?"

If not a good time: "No problem. Is there a better day, and is this the best number?" Log it, move on.

## One-line reason for the call

**Clinical track:**
"We built a local-first tool that clears report backlog. It drafts your evaluation reports in your own voice from your case file, keeps every diagnostic decision signed by you, and never sends patient data off your machine. Most of the psychologists we talk to are losing evenings to report writing, so I wanted to see if that is you."

**Forensic track:**
"We built a local-first tool for evaluators where defensibility is the point. Patient data stays on your machine, every diagnostic decision is signed by you and audit-logged for discovery, and it drafts court-ready reports in your voice in a fraction of the writing time. I wanted to see if report turnaround is a pain point for you."

## Discovery (one or two questions, then listen)

- "How much of your week goes to writing up evaluations right now?"
- "Are you doing forensic work, clinical assessment, or both?" (confirms track)
- Optional: "What are you using today, dictation, templates, something else?"

## The differentiator (only if they engage)

- "The part people care about most is that it is local-first. Nothing leaves your machine, so there is no cloud PHI exposure to worry about."
- "And the clinician stays the author of record. The software never diagnoses; it drafts after you have signed the decisions. That is a hard line for us."
- Founder urgency (only when true and natural): "We are signing the first hundred practices at a founder rate that holds for life. Those seats are the reason I am calling now rather than in six months."

## The ask (pick the highest they will give)

1. "Could I put fifteen minutes on your calendar to show you? I can work around patient hours."
2. "If a call is tough, what is the best email? I will send a three-minute demo video and the founder link, no obligation."
3. "Can I check back in a couple of weeks?"

## Close

"Appreciate the time, [Dr. Last name]. You will get [the calendar invite / the email] from me today. It comes from boreasworkflow.com, so it will not look like spam."

## Voicemail (under 20 seconds, leave the URL slowly)

"Hi [Dr. Last name], this is Robert Irwin with Boreas Workflow, we make local-first software for psychological evaluation that drafts reports in your own voice and keeps patient data on your machine. No rush; if it is useful, the demo is at boreasworkflow.com, that is b-o-r-e-a-s workflow dot com. Thanks, and have a good one."

---

## Objection handling

- **"I don't use AI for clinical work."** "Agreed, and that is the design. The software does not diagnose and does not decide. You make and sign every clinical judgment first; it only helps you write it up afterward, in your voice. Nothing is sent anywhere."
- **"Is my patient data safe?"** "It never leaves your computer. Storage is encrypted on your machine, and anything that would ever touch an outside service is de-identified first. There is no cloud case storage at all."
- **"I already have templates / a system."** "Makes sense. This starts from your templates and fills the first draft from the case file, so it is less a new system than a faster path through the one you have. Fifteen minutes will tell you if it saves you time."
- **"How much?"** "Pro is 299 a month, and the first hundred practices lock a 199 founder rate for life. There is a ten-day free trial, no card, so you can see it on a real case before deciding."
- **"Send me something."** Great outcome. Capture the email, confirm spelling, log it for the email sequence, and send the demo video + founder link same day.
- **"Not interested / take me off the list."** "Understood, I will not call again. Thanks for your time." Log as do-not-call immediately.

---

## Logging (after each call)

Record for every dial: outcome (demo booked / email captured / callback / not interested / no answer / voicemail / do-not-call / bad number), track, email if captured, and any note. Do-not-call and bad-number flags feed straight back into the list so nobody gets dialed twice. Emails captured feed the solo email sequence.
