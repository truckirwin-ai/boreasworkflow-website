Features

Every feature is in service of clinical judgment.

Boreas Workflow is opinionated. It makes the right thing easy and the wrong thing impossible. AI organizes evidence. The clinician makes every decision the referral, the court, or the payer cares about. Built for forensic work. Rigorous enough for clinical evaluation.

The gate

Decision gates stop the AI from diagnosing

The Diagnostician agent reads everything the clinician has gathered and proposes an evidence map. For every diagnosis it considers relevant, it shows the DSM-5-TR criteria, the ICD-10 code, and the supporting quotes pulled from the case file. The agent does not diagnose.

The clinician renders, defers, or rejects. The clinician can add diagnoses the agent missed. The clinician can override on every line. Reasoning is captured in a clinical notes field, timestamped, signed, and written to the audit log.

The Writer agent refuses to run until at least one diagnosis has been rendered. No skip. No AI-only path. The doctor diagnoses, or the report does not exist.

Privacy and PII

Names never leave the laptop

Boreas Workflow runs Microsoft Presidio and a spaCy NER model locally in a sidecar process. PII detection, redaction, and rehydration all happen on the clinician's machine. Names, addresses, phone numbers, and identifiers are never sent to a remote service for detection.

When the AI agents run against remote model providers, Boreas Workflow redacts first and rehydrates on the clinician's side. The upstream model sees tokens, not patients.

Discoverable record

Every decision, reproducible on demand

Every field entered, every test scored, every record ingested, every agent run, every diagnosis rendered, every draft revision, and every signature is written to an append-only audit log.

When a case goes to court, or a payer audits a clinical evaluation, the clinician exports a package that includes the intake, test raw scores, interview notes, source documents, agent outputs, clinical decisions with rationale, report revisions, and cryptographic hashes for every file. Cross-examination becomes a matter of reading the record, not reconstructing it.

04

Diagnostics The gate

The case enters Diagnostics. This is where Boreas Workflow's design becomes uncompromising.

The Diagnostician agent reads everything the clinician has gathered and proposes an evidence map. For every diagnosis it considers relevant, it shows the DSM-5-TR criteria, the ICD-10 code, and the specific evidence from the case file that supports or contradicts each criterion.

The agent does not diagnose. It cannot. It assembles evidence and presents it to the clinician. The clinician, and only the clinician, decides which diagnoses are rendered, which are deferred, and which are rejected. The clinician can add diagnoses the agent missed. The clinician can override the agent on every line. Reasoning is captured in a clinical notes field, timestamped, signed, and written to the audit log.

Until the clinician makes at least one diagnostic decision, the Writer agent refuses to run. There is no skip. There is no AI-only path.

05

Reports

With the clinical decisions made, the Writer agent drafts the report. It pulls language from the intake, the structured interview notes, the test results, and only the diagnoses the clinician has personally rendered. The Editor agent reviews the draft for internal consistency, tone, and citation accuracy.

The clinician reviews the draft, edits any language they disagree with, and attests. Attestation is a cryptographically signed, timestamped event tied to the clinician's identity. The report is locked. The audit log records every change from intake to signature.

If this case ends up in court, the clinician can reproduce the evidentiary chain on demand. Every record. Every test. Every decision. Every revision. Every signature.

Before you install

Answers to the questions clinicians ask first

Do I need an internet connection?

Only to activate the license and to call the remote AI model provider for the Writer, Editor, Ingestor, and Diagnostician agents. Intake, testing, interviews, PII detection, and the audit log run without network. If you disconnect during case work, the app keeps working and queues nothing to a remote server because there is no Boreas Workflow server for case data.

About

We build the evidence engine. The clinician still diagnoses.

Boreas Workflow is a desktop IDE for forensic and clinical psychology, built by Foundry SMB in Colorado. We publish software that makes the record defensible on cross-examination and keeps the decisions where they belong, with the licensed clinician.

Thesis

Evidence engine, not a decision engine.

The easiest way to ship an AI tool for clinicians is to let the model draft the diagnosis and ask the human to click Approve. We refuse to build that. It is ethically wrong, it is legally unsupportable, and it is obvious to any attorney cross-examining the report. Boreas Workflow is the opposite posture.

The clinician decides.

The Writer agent cannot draft a line until the clinician renders, defers, or rejects each proposed diagnosis, with signed reasoning written to the audit log.

Every fact is sourced.

Intake fields typed by the clinician. Scores timestamped by scorer. Quotes tied to transcripts. Orphan claims do not make it into the report.

Reproducible on discovery.

Cryptographic attestation on every signed report. Reconstruct the full decision trail in minutes when the defense asks for it.

How we work

Small team, weekly releases, no surprises.

Local-first by design

Boreas Workflow is a desktop application. Clinical records live on the clinician's workstation. Our servers handle fulfillment and a daily license check. PHI does not transit our infrastructure in the default configuration. See the HIPAA notice for how we handle the exceptions.

No trackers, no profiling

The marketing site runs Cloudflare Web Analytics without cookies. The desktop app ships no telemetry. We do not use customer work product to train models. We do not sell or share personal information. Full details are in the Privacy Policy.

Shipping cadence

We release on a weekly cadence when changes are ready and never on a Friday afternoon. Release notes land in-app and by email to subscribers. Security fixes ship out of band with a public advisory. Responsible disclosure has a clear safe harbor. See Security.

Support is a human

Every ticket is read by someone on the team. Tier-based response targets are set in writing in the Support SLA. Enterprise customers have a named technical contact. There is no AI reply bot.

Foundry will not pursue legal action, issue a takedown, or refer to law enforcement any researcher who in good faith complies with this policy. "Good faith" means: you stay within scope, you stop as soon as you have enough information to confirm the issue, you do not access or modify data that is not yours, you do not exfiltrate data beyond what is needed to demonstrate the issue, you do not disrupt service or users, and you give us a reasonable time to fix before public disclosure.

4. What to avoid

  • Testing against production customer accounts without written permission.
  • Automated scanners that generate high volumes of traffic.
  • Accessing, copying, or destroying data that is not yours.
  • Public disclosure before a fix is shipped, unless we fail to respond within thirty days or explicitly agree.

5. Disclosure

Once a fix is available, we will credit the researcher by name or handle, if they wish, in release notes or a security advisory. A formal bug-bounty program with monetary rewards is not offered at this time.

1. Local-first posture

Boreas Workflow is designed so that protected health information (PHI), as defined in 45 CFR 160.103, remains on the clinician's workstation. PHI can reach us through narrow channels: support tickets that include identifying details, crash reports if Customer enables them, or future features that Customer elects to use that route content through our infrastructure. For Practice and Enterprise tiers, Foundry will enter into a Business Associate Agreement ("BAA") on request to cover those incidental flows.

Solo
No BAA available. Solo customers must not include PHI in support tickets or any other communication with Foundry.
Practice

Enterprise

4. Subcontractors

If a BAA is in force, Foundry flows down HIPAA obligations to its subcontractors that may come into contact with PHI, including Cloudflare, Stripe, and Resend, each of which offers a BAA. Foundry maintains administrative, physical, and technical safeguards consistent with 45 CFR 164.308, 164.310, and 164.312.

5. Breach notification

If Foundry discovers a breach of unsecured PHI, Foundry will notify the affected Covered Entity without unreasonable delay and in no case later than sixty days after discovery, consistent with 45 CFR 164.410.

6. Customer responsibilities

Customer, as the Covered Entity, is responsible for: lawful collection of PHI, patient authorizations, minimum-necessary disclosures, workstation security, user access controls on the clinician's device, and reporting breaches that occur on Customer-controlled systems. Boreas Workflow's decision gates and audit log help, but do not discharge, these obligations.

7. Requesting a BAA

Email legal@boreasworkflow.com from the account email on file. Include the legal name of the Covered Entity, the authorized signer, and the tier. Our standard BAA is based on the HHS sample BAA and can be sent for signature through our e-signature provider.
