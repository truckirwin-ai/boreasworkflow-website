# Trademark Prep Packet: CRESTONE

For the forensic and clinical psychology software product (the Boreas rename), documented as the backup to
Boreas. Prepared June 2, 2026, for handoff to a trademark attorney. Preparatory research, not legal advice.

## Applicant and mark

- Applicant: Foundry SMB LLC (Colorado).
- Mark: CRESTONE.
- Format: standard-character word mark first; stylized/logo later.
- Filing basis: likely Section 1(b), intent to use.

## Recommended classes and goods/services wording

Class 9 (downloadable software):
"Downloadable software for managing psychological and forensic evaluation workflows, including case intake,
record organization, clinical documentation, and report drafting."

Class 42 (software as a service):
"Software as a service (SaaS) featuring software for managing psychological and forensic evaluation
workflows; providing temporary use of non-downloadable software for clinical documentation, case
management, and report generation."

Class 44 note: likely NOT required (software, not clinical care). Confirm with attorney.

## Knockout search summary (preliminary, public records)

- Crestone Capital LLC (Boulder): holds a registered CRESTONE mark, almost certainly in financial
  services (Class 36). Different field from software, but a strong established registration on the bare word.
- Crestone, Inc. / Crestone Pharma (Boulder): active clinical-stage biopharmaceutical company. Likely
  touches Class 5 (pharmaceuticals) and possibly Class 42 (scientific research).
- Crestone Recovery and Crestone Wellness: behavioral-health treatment providers (Class 44 services,
  likely common-law / unregistered).
- Domains crestone.com and crestonehealth.com are already registered by third parties.

## Risk read

More crowded than Boreas in exactly the health-adjacent classes. A biopharma touching Class 5 and
research Class 42, behavioral-health service users in Class 44, plus a registered financial-services mark on
the bare word. Software Classes 9 and 42 may still be obtainable, but the relatedness picture (especially the
biopharma's possible Class 42 research activities and the behavioral-health Class 44 users) needs careful
attorney analysis. Brand confusion and search-result crowding are higher than for Boreas.

## Supporting assets

- Domains to secure: crestoneclinical.com (primary), crestoneworkflow.com, getcrestone.com.
  Note that crestone.com and crestonehealth.com are unavailable.
- Specimen plan: post-launch web app and marketing site showing CRESTONE as the product brand.

## Attorney handoff checklist

1. Full clearance search (federal, state, common law) on CRESTONE in Classes 9 and 42.
2. Relatedness analysis versus Crestone Capital (Class 36), Crestone Inc. biopharma (Classes 5 and 42),
   and the behavioral-health Crestone service providers (Class 44).
3. Confirm Section 1(b) intent-to-use filing; applicant Foundry SMB LLC.
4. Assess whether the biopharma's research-class activity creates a meaningful Class 42 obstacle.
5. Refine goods and services wording above.

## Position relative to Boreas

Boreas is the recommended lead: cleaner trademark field and full domain control. Crestone is retained here
as a viable backup if the attorney clears Classes 9 and 42 and the client accepts coexistence with the
existing Crestone financial, pharma, and behavioral-health names.
