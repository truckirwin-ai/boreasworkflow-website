Shape of fit

Two shapes. Pick the one your work needs.

Each axis is a priority for forensic and high-stakes clinical work. Boreas Workflow leads on the axes where the record has to hold up. Cloud-first competitors lead on cloud and portal breadth. Read the shape, not the area.

Boreas Workflow Typical cloud AI drafter

Boreas Workflow trades cloud and portal breadth for clinical control. The question is whether the shape lines up with what your work demands.

  • Forensic defensibilitySigned diagnostic gates and an integrity hash on the final report. Cloud drafters do not enforce either.
  • Audit traceabilityHash-chained log of every material decision, exportable for testimony. Drafters keep cloud-side activity logs.
  • Clinical workflow integrityThe writer is blocked until the clinician signs the diagnosis. Drafters draft on demand.
  • Local data controlThe case workspace lives on your machine. PHI is redacted before any model call.
  • Assessment breadthComparable. Boreas Workflow ships 43 instruments and imports more.
  • Integrations / portalThe deliberate concession. Cloud SaaS leads on EHR connectors and patient portals.

Decision pressure

The deposition test.

Imagine opposing counsel has your report and is asking how it was generated. Here is what each category produces under that pressure.

"Doctor, walk me through how this report was created."

This is the moment that decides whether your engagement holds up. Your defense rests on the evidence behind your decisions.

Cloud AI drafter

A cloud activity log, transcript fragments, the prompts you typed, and the final draft. Diagnoses can be generated by the model on demand. There is no signed clinical decision separate from the prose.

Boreas Workflow

Clinician-signed diagnostic decisions with timestamps. Hash-chained audit trail across every material event. Local PHI handling, with redaction before any model call. Report integrity hash on the final document. Exportable testimony folder.

What Boreas Workflow isn't.

Boreas Workflow makes these tradeoffs deliberately.

  • Not a patient portal.Patient-facing scheduling, intake, and messaging belong elsewhere in your stack.
  • Not an EHR.Use your EHR for chart of record. Export the Boreas Workflow report into it.
  • Not the cheapest drafter.For volume drafting in low-stakes settings, cloud SaaS is the right buy.
  • Image and handwritten input is limited.Roadmap item. Today, intake forms work better as typed text or supported imports.
