Defensible use of AI in psychological assessment

AI tools are arriving in assessment practice faster than the guidance on using them safely. This session covers the questions that matter before you let any AI product near an evaluation: where PHI actually goes, what opposing counsel will ask about your process, what your documentation needs to show, and what belongs in your informed consent language. Practical, vendor-honest, and grounded in how evaluations are actually challenged.

What we cover

Sixty minutes, four questions

Where does the data go? The difference between cloud-processed and local-first PHI handling, what a BAA does and does not cover, and the questions to put to any vendor before patient data touches their product.

What will opposing counsel ask? How AI-assisted work product gets challenged: authorship, reliability, the basis of opinions, and the discovery requests already appearing in cases. What an audit trail needs to contain for your process to hold up.

What does your documentation need to show? Standards for documenting AI assistance in reports and records, for both courtroom scrutiny and payer review. Who decided what, and where the record proves it.

What do you tell the people you evaluate? Informed consent and notification language for AI-assisted workflows, with examples you can adapt.
