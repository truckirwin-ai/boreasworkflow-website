Why it moves the backlog

The bottleneck is the writing, not the testing.

You can administer and score faster than you can write. The report is where evals stack up, and the backlog is what caps how many patients you see and how much the practice bills. Boreas takes the scored case file (intake, rating scales, instrument results, interview notes) and produces a structured first draft in your own language, so the writing stops being the throttle.

The documentation comes out criterion-mapped and source-cited, the way payer reviewers want to see it. Fewer requests for records, fewer rewrites, cleaner authorization.

Why the data stays put

Local-first, so the file never leaves your office.

Patient data lives on your machine in an encrypted case store (AES-256), never in our cloud. When an assistant needs to reason over case content, identifiers are stripped first. You are the author of record, and every diagnostic decision is rendered and signed by you before a line of the report is drafted. The clinician diagnoses. Always.
